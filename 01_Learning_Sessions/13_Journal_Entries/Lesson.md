\# What is a Journal Entry?



A Journal Entry is the formal accounting record of a financial transaction.



Every journal entry follows double-entry accounting.



Every entry must balance.



Total Debits = Total Credits.



\---



\# Parts of a Journal Entry



Every journal entry contains:



\- Date

\- Accounts

\- Debit Amount

\- Credit Amount

\- Description

\- Reference (optional)

\# Why Journal Entries Matter



Journal Entries become the foundation of:



\- General Ledger

\- Trial Balance

\- Financial Statements



Without journal entries, accounting reports cannot exist.
