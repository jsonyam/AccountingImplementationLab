\# Lesson 05 – Chart of Accounts



\# Learning Objectives



By the end of this lesson, I should be able to:



\- Understand what a Chart of Accounts (COA) is.

\- Explain why every business needs a Chart of Accounts.

\- Identify the five main account categories.

\- Understand how QuickBooks and Xero organize accounts.

\- Design a basic Chart of Accounts for a small business.



\---



\# What is a Chart of Accounts?



A Chart of Accounts (COA) is the complete list of accounts a business uses to record financial transactions.



Think of it as the filing cabinet for every accounting entry.



Without a Chart of Accounts, transactions have nowhere to be recorded.



\---



\# Why is it Important?



Every sale...



Every expense...



Every payment...



Every invoice...



must be posted to an account.



The Chart of Accounts keeps financial data organized.



\---



\# The Five Main Categories



\## Assets



Resources the business owns.



Examples



\- Cash

\- Bank

\- Inventory

\- Equipment

\- Accounts Receivable



\---



\## Liabilities



Money the business owes.



Examples



\- Accounts Payable

\- Loans

\- Credit Cards

\- Taxes Payable



\---



\## Owner's Equity



Owner's investment and accumulated profit.



Examples



\- Owner's Capital

\- Retained Earnings



\---



\## Revenue



Income earned by the business.



Examples



\- Sales Revenue

\- Service Revenue

\- Shipping Income



\---



\## Expenses



Costs incurred while operating the business.



Examples



\- Rent

\- Utilities

\- Advertising

\- Salaries

\- Internet

\# ERP Perspective



ERP systems use the Chart of Accounts to determine where financial transactions are posted.



Every purchasing, inventory, sales, and payroll transaction eventually posts to one or more accounts.



\---



\# QuickBooks



QuickBooks includes a default Chart of Accounts.



Implementation Consultants usually customize it to match the client's business.



\---



\# Xero



Same concept.



Different interface.



Businesses often modify the default accounts during implementation.



\---



\# Shopify



Shopify itself does not maintain a full Chart of Accounts.



When integrated with QuickBooks or Xero, Shopify sales are mapped to specific revenue and asset accounts.



\---



\# Consultant Perspective



One of the first implementation tasks is reviewing or designing the client's Chart of Accounts.



A poorly designed COA leads to poor financial reporting.



\---



\# Key Takeaways



\- Every business needs a Chart of Accounts.

\- Every transaction posts to one or more accounts.

\- The COA forms the foundation of financial reporting.

\- Consultants often customize the COA during implementation.
