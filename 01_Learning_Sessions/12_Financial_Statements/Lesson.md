\# Lesson 12 – Financial Statements



\# Learning Objectives



By the end of this lesson, I should be able to:



\- Understand the purpose of financial statements.

\- Identify the three primary financial statements.

\- Explain how financial statements relate to accounting transactions.

\- Understand how QuickBooks and Xero generate reports.

\- Explain why financial statements are important to business owners.



\---



\# What are Financial Statements?



Financial Statements summarize the financial performance and financial position of a business.



They answer questions like:



\- Is the business profitable?

\- How much cash does the business have?

\- How much does the business owe?

\- Is the business growing?



\---



\# The Three Primary Financial Statements



\## 1. Balance Sheet



Shows what the business owns and owes at a specific date.



Formula:



Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity



\---



\## 2. Income Statement



Also called the Profit \& Loss Statement.



Shows:



Revenue



\-



Expenses



=



Net Profit



\---



\## 3. Cash Flow Statement



Shows how cash moved during the period.



Cash In



Cash Out



Ending Cash



\---



\# How Transactions Affect Reports



Every accounting transaction updates one or more financial statements.



Example:



Customer Sale



↓



Revenue increases



↓



Income Statement updates



↓



Cash or Accounts Receivable updates



↓



Balance Sheet updates



\---



\# ERP Perspective



ERP systems generate operational transactions.



Accounting converts them into financial statements.



Management uses these reports to make decisions.



\---



\# QuickBooks



QuickBooks generates:



\- Balance Sheet

\- Profit \& Loss

\- Cash Flow

\- Trial Balance

\- AR Aging

\- AP Aging



\---



\# Xero



Xero provides similar reports with real-time financial information.



\---



\# Shopify



Shopify reports operational sales.



QuickBooks and Xero convert those sales into financial statements.
