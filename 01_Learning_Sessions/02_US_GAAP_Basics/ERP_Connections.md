\# Where US GAAP Fits



US GAAP does not manage business operations.



Instead, it defines \*\*how financial transactions should be recognized and reported\*\*.



Examples include:



\- Revenue recognition

\- Expense recognition

\- Asset valuation

\- Financial statement presentation



\---



\# ERP Perspective



ERP systems manage operational activities such as:



\- Purchasing

\- Inventory

\- Manufacturing

\- Sales

\- Warehouse

\- Customer Management



Accounting systems transform these operational events into financial records that comply with accounting standards.



\---



\# Consultant Perspective



As an Implementation Consultant, you must understand:



\- The business process

\- The operational workflow

\- The accounting impact



Every operational event eventually becomes a financial transaction.

\# Key Takeaway



US GAAP provides the accounting rules.



ERP manages operations.



QuickBooks and Xero record financial transactions.



Shopify manages ecommerce.



Together they create a complete business process from operations to financial reporting.
