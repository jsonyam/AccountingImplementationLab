\# What is US GAAP?



US GAAP stands for \*\*Generally Accepted Accounting Principles\*\*.



It is a standardized set of accounting rules used in the United States to ensure that financial information is accurate, consistent, and comparable across businesses.



Think of US GAAP as the rulebook for financial reporting.



\---



\# Why is US GAAP Important?



Without accounting standards, every company could record transactions differently.



For example:



Company A records revenue today.



Company B records the same revenue next month.



Investors would not be able to compare their financial performance fairly.



US GAAP provides consistency so financial reports can be trusted.



\---



\# Who Uses US GAAP?



US GAAP is commonly used by:



\- US companies

\- Publicly traded companies

\- Businesses reporting to US regulators

\- Many multinational companies with US operations



Private companies may also choose to follow US GAAP.



\---



\# US GAAP vs IFRS



There are two major accounting standards worldwide.



| US GAAP | IFRS |

|---------|------|

| Primarily used in the United States | Used in many other countries |

| Rules-based | Principles-based |

| Governed by FASB | Governed by IASB |



As an implementation consultant, it is important to know which accounting standard your client follows.



\---



\# How QuickBooks and Xero Relate to US GAAP



QuickBooks and Xero are accounting systems.



They do not create accounting standards.



Instead, they help businesses record transactions in accordance with the accounting rules the business follows.



The software supports the accounting process, but the accounting policies come from the business.



\---



\# ERP Perspective



ERP systems manage operational processes such as:



\- Purchasing

\- Inventory

\- Sales

\- Manufacturing

\- Warehouse



Accounting systems convert those operational activities into financial records that comply with accounting standards.



\---



\# Shopify Perspective



Shopify records ecommerce activity.



Examples:



\- Orders

\- Products

\- Customers

\- Payments



QuickBooks or Xero converts those business events into accounting transactions.



\---



\# Real Business Example



A customer buys a bottle of perfume for ₱5,000.



Shopify records:



\- Customer

\- Order

\- Payment



QuickBooks records:



\- Revenue

\- Cash received

\- Taxes (if applicable)



Financial reports are then generated using the company's accounting policies.



\---



\# Key Takeaways



\- US GAAP is a set of accounting standards.

\- Accounting standards create consistency.

\- QuickBooks and Xero help businesses apply accounting practices.

\- Shopify is not an accounting standard.

\- ERP systems provide operational data that eventually becomes accounting data.
