\# Lesson 04 – Debits and Credits



\# Learning Objectives



By the end of this lesson, I should be able to:



\- Understand what debits and credits are.

\- Know why every transaction requires both a debit and a credit.

\- Identify the normal balance of common accounts.

\- Explain how QuickBooks and Xero automatically create debit and credit entries.

\- Apply debits and credits to real business scenarios.



\---



\# What are Debits and Credits?



Debits and credits are the two sides of every accounting transaction.



Every transaction affects at least two accounts.



The total debits must always equal the total credits.



This keeps the Accounting Equation balanced.



\---



\# Debit Does NOT Mean Increase



One of the biggest beginner mistakes is thinking:



Debit = Increase



Credit = Decrease



This is NOT always true.



Whether a debit increases or decreases an account depends on the account type.



\---



\# Normal Balance



| Account | Debit | Credit |

|---------|------|-------|

| Assets | Increase | Decrease |

| Liabilities | Decrease | Increase |

| Owner's Equity | Decrease | Increase |

| Revenue | Decrease | Increase |

| Expenses | Increase | Decrease |



Remember:



\*\*Assets and Expenses increase with Debits.\*\*



Everything else increases with Credits.

\# ERP Perspective



ERP users usually don't enter debits and credits manually.



They perform business activities.



The ERP creates the accounting entries automatically.



\---



\# QuickBooks



When you create an invoice...



QuickBooks automatically creates the debit and credit entries.



Users rarely see them.



\---



\# Xero



Same concept.



Different interface.



Same accounting rules.



\---



\# Shopify



Shopify records the sale.



QuickBooks or Xero records the accounting entry.



\---



\# Key Takeaways



Every transaction has:



\- One or more Debits

\- One or more Credits



Total Debits must always equal Total Credits.
