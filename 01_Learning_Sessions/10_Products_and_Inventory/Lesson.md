\# Lesson 10 – Products and Inventory



\# Learning Objectives



By the end of this lesson, I should be able to:



\- Understand inventory as an accounting asset.

\- Understand product master data.

\- Explain inventory movements.

\- Understand inventory valuation.

\- Explain how QuickBooks and Xero manage inventory.

\- Understand the consultant's role during inventory implementation.



\---



\# What is Inventory?



Inventory consists of products a business owns for resale.



Inventory is classified as a Current Asset because it will eventually be sold to generate revenue.



\---



\# Product Master Data



Each product normally contains:



\- SKU

\- Product Name

\- Category

\- Unit Cost

\- Selling Price

\- Quantity On Hand

\- Reorder Point

\- Supplier

\- Tax Code



Accurate master data is essential for reliable inventory reporting.



\---



\# Inventory Movements



Inventory increases when:



\- Purchased

\- Returned by customers

\- Adjusted upward

\- Initial stock entered



Inventory decreases when:



\- Sold

\- Damaged

\- Lost

\- Adjusted downward



\---



\# Inventory Valuation



Inventory has financial value.



Example:



100 Bottles



Unit Cost = ₱500



Inventory Value



100 × ₱500 = ₱50,000



This value appears on the Balance Sheet.



\---



\# Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)



When inventory is sold:



Inventory decreases.



Cost of Goods Sold increases.



Revenue and COGS together determine Gross Profit.



\---
