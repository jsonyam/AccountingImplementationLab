\# Lesson 09 – Bank Reconciliation



\# Learning Objectives



By the end of this lesson, I should be able to:



\- Understand what bank reconciliation is.

\- Identify common causes of differences between bank records and accounting records.

\- Understand how QuickBooks and Xero perform reconciliation.

\- Understand the consultant's role in reconciliation setup.



\---



\# What is Bank Reconciliation?



Bank Reconciliation is the process of comparing the accounting records with the bank statement.



Its purpose is to ensure both balances are correct.



\---



\# Why is Bank Reconciliation Important?



It helps identify:



\- Missing transactions

\- Duplicate entries

\- Bank charges

\- Interest income

\- Recording errors

\- Fraud



Businesses should reconcile their bank accounts regularly.



\---



\# Reconciliation Process



Accounting Records



↓



Bank Statement



↓



Compare Transactions



↓



Identify Differences



↓



Correct Errors



↓



Balances Match



\---



\# Common Differences



Outstanding Checks



Checks issued but not yet cleared by the bank.



Outstanding Deposits



Deposits recorded by the business but not yet processed by the bank.



Bank Charges



Fees deducted by the bank.



Interest Income



Interest added by the bank.



Recording Errors



Incorrect amounts or duplicate entries.
