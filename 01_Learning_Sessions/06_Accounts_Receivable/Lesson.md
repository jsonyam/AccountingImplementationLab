\- Understand what Accounts Receivable (AR) is.

\---



\# What is Accounts Receivable?



Accounts Receivable (AR) is money customers owe the business for goods or services already delivered.



The business has earned the revenue but has not yet received the cash.



\---



\# Why is AR an Asset?



AR represents money the business expects to collect.



Since it provides future economic benefit, it is classified as an Asset.

\# Real Example



ABC Company purchases ₱15,000 worth of perfume.



You allow payment within 30 days.



Today



Invoice = ₱15,000



Cash = ₱0



Accounts Receivable = ₱15,000



Thirty days later



Customer pays.



Cash increases.



Accounts Receivable decreases.
