\# What is Accounting?



Accounting is the process of recording, organizing, summarizing, and reporting a company's financial transactions.



Its purpose is to answer questions like:



\- How much money does the business have?

\- How much profit did it make?

\- Who owes us money?

\- Who do we owe money to?

\- Can we afford to buy more inventory?



Without accounting, a business is simply guessing.



\---



\# Why Businesses Need Accounting



Every business needs accounting because it helps owners:



\- Track income

\- Track expenses

\- Monitor cash flow

\- Pay taxes correctly

\- Measure profitability

\- Make better business decisions



Accounting turns business activities into financial information.



\---



\# Bookkeeping vs Accounting



\## Bookkeeping



Bookkeeping focuses on recording transactions.



Examples:



\- Sales

\- Purchases

\- Payments

\- Receipts

\- Bank deposits



\---



\## Accounting



Accounting analyzes the information recorded by bookkeeping.



Examples:



\- Financial reports

\- Profit analysis

\- Budget planning

\- Tax preparation

\- Business recommendations



Think of bookkeeping as collecting data.



Think of accounting as using that data to make decisions.



\---



\# Where QuickBooks and Xero Fit



QuickBooks and Xero are accounting software.



They help businesses:



\- Record transactions

\- Manage customers

\- Manage suppliers

\- Create invoices

\- Record bills

\- Track inventory

\- Reconcile bank accounts

\- Generate financial reports



They automate bookkeeping while providing accounting reports.



\---



\# Relationship with ERP Systems



ERP systems handle business operations.



Examples:



\- Purchasing

\- Inventory

\- Sales

\- Warehouse

\- Manufacturing

\- CRM



Accounting software handles the financial side.



Many companies connect ERP systems with QuickBooks or Xero.



\---



\# Relationship with Shopify



Shopify manages ecommerce.



Examples:



\- Products

\- Orders

\- Customers

\- Payments



However, Shopify is not a full accounting system.



Businesses usually integrate Shopify with:



\- QuickBooks Online

\- Xero



Sales from Shopify become accounting transactions inside those systems.



\---



\# Real Business Example



Customer buys a perfume from a Shopify store.



Flow:



Customer

↓



Shopify Order

↓



Payment Received

↓



QuickBooks / Xero

↓



Sales Recorded



↓



Financial Reports Updated



Everything starts from one sale but ends as accounting information.



\---



\# Key Takeaways



\- Accounting records financial activities.

\- Bookkeeping records transactions.

\- Accounting interprets business performance.

\- QuickBooks and Xero are accounting platforms.

\- Shopify manages ecommerce.

\- ERP manages business operations.

\- These systems often work together.
